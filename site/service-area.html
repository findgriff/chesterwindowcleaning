Service area · CH1–CH5

Where I clean.

CH1–CH5: Chester and the closer parts of Deeside.

My round is built around Chester and the closer parts of Deeside, so I'm never more than a few minutes from the next job. That's deliberate — less time driving means lower prices and a visit you can rely on every 4–6 weeks. If your postcode starts CH1, CH2, CH3, CH4 or CH5, you're on the round.

Window cleaning in Chester (CH1–CH3)

The core of the round: Chester city centre, Hoole, Boughton and Newton on the east side, Handbridge and the Lache side of the river, and Upton, Mollington and Saughall to the north. Out east I cover Vicars Cross, Christleton, Mickle Trafford and across to Tarvin, Kelsall and parts of Tarporley. A lot of Chester housing is Victorian terraces and 1930s semis with awkward upstairs bays — the water-fed pole reaches all of it from the ground, no ladders against your brickwork.

Window cleaning in Saltney & Broughton (CH4)

Over the border into Flintshire: Saltney, Broughton and Hawarden. Newer estates here often have velux windows and conservatories — both simple add-ons on the round, priced on the pricing page.

Window cleaning in Queensferry & Connah's Quay (CH5)

The Deeside end of the round: Queensferry, Connah's Quay and the villages between. Same service, same published prices, same text the night before.

Outside CH1–CH5?

If you're outside this area I'd be travelling more than cleaning, so I have to say no — sorry. The chat bot checks your postcode in seconds, so you'll know straight away.
